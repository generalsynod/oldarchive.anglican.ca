Lenten Reflection Podcast

The Anglican Church of Canada has created a series of reflections available for download during Lent, February 21st through April 8th, 2007. These meditations give you a chance to consider questions you might otherwise not think about: Are you taking care of yourself? Are you being truly open and caring with your loved ones? What is important to you? Are you finding meaning in your day to day life?

All the reflections have been created by Philip Wadham, an Anglican priest in Victoria BC. Philip has been ministering to Canadians and others overseas for more than 25years. He has worked as a regional mission coordinator for the General Synod of the Anglican Church of Canada.

This podcast is a joint experiment of Anglican Video and the web department of General Synod. Please send your thoughts and comments to webmanager@national.anglican.ca.

What is a podcast?

A podcast is an audio that is distributed by subscription over the Internet using syndication feeds, for playback on mobile devices and personal computers.

How do I use these files?

The weekly audio reflections can be downloaded free as MP3s. You download each weeks file as a ZIP file, or subscribe to the podcasts using iTunes (for free) or via an RSS download.

If you download an entire week in a ZIP file, you then need to unzip the files. This is done by right-clicking on the ZIP file and selecting "Extract All".

You then transfer the files to your MP3 player just as you would any other file.

Do I need an MP3 player?

No. If you wish to listen to these files on your computer, just use the built in audio-player (above).

How does podcasting work?

The producers of a podcast put MP3 files on their web site so that users can download them. In addition, they include on their site a single, small file called an RSS feed. Basically, this is a list of the files that are available, along with a few other details. Every time a new episode is added to the site, they also update the "feed" to include it in the list. This RSS feed is the file that the podcast-receiving programs in users' computers will read at regular intervals, to see if new episodes are available. If your podcast-receiving software spots an episode that it hasn't downloaded, it goes ahead and gets it (or it may ask you first, depending on the settings you have chosen).

To subscribe to a podcast, the only information you need to give your computer is the location (the URL) of the "feed". Once it has this, it can do all the rest.

But first, you need to have some kind of podcast-receiving software installed on your computer.

What software do I need?

There is a wide choice of podcast-receiving software, most of it free, available on the web to download and install on your computer. Some popular software links:
