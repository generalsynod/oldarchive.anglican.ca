&homeText=<font color="#FFFFFF" size="14" FACE="Arial"><p>Archbishop Andrew Hutchison, Primate of the Anglican Church of Canada, joined indigenous Anglicans from across Canada at the Fifth Anglican Indigenous Sacred Circle, held last year at Pinawa, Man.</p>
<p>In this webcast, Archbishop Hutchison takes a closer look at the history of the Sacred Circle, and describes the highlights of the most recent one.</p>
<p>More information on the Fifth Anglican Indigenous Sacred Circle is <u><a href="http://www.anglican.ca/about/committees/acip/sc2005/index.htm" target="_blank">available online</a></u>. (opens in a new window)</p></font>&

&story1vText=<FONT FACE="Arial" SIZE="14" COLOR="#000000"><p>The Sacred Circle is a gathering of Anglican Indigenous people from across Canada. The first was held in Fort Qu'Appelle, Sask in 1988.</p>
<p>Earlier circles have brought to light the legacy of relationships between the Anglican Church of Canada and its indigenous members, with special focus on the legacy of Indian Residential Schools.</p>

&story2vText=<FONT FACE="Arial" SIZE="14" COLOR="#000000"><p>This circle was the first one Archbishop Hutchison attended as Primate.</p>
<p>The major discussion at this circle was a proposal for a National Indigenous Bishop. This proposal was brought to the circle, where members of the circle including youth members approved it unanimously. Bishops in attendance then expressed their support.</p>
<p>More information about the National Indigenous Bishop can be found <u><a href="http://www.anglican.ca/about/committees/acip/documents/acip-nib.pdf" target="_blank">here</a></u>. (link opens in a new window)</p></font>&
