So as I moved from place to place, I have found the same thing.Â  There seems to be a deep lack of love and God amongst the people.Â  I have noticed that the Church lacks the ability to reach out to those who are suffering, in pain, having difficulties, and going through a crisis.Â  When I have personally gone through a crisis, it seems that the Church has run in the opposite direction and wish not to have anything to do with me.Â  Perhaps that may be because I am a priest and no one knows how to handle a priest who is experiencing any kind of distress.Â  I must admit though, the Rector of this local parish is the first one, representing the Church, has reached out to me.Â  But I am not sure that itÂ was for the right reasons.Â  I think it was to remind me that, as a priest and pastor, I cannot do anything in the Church.Â  I know others have only experienced the wrath of the Church.Â The Church is suppose to be, and pronounces that it is, loving and caring and compassionate.Â  I have yet to experience this side of the Church.

I have just completed writing my story from the time that I entered training for ordained ministry to the present.Â  I could not believe how many times that I have tried to reach out to the Church for comfort and solace only to be pushed away.Â  God seems to be so absent from the Church.Â  The Church meets each week to do the ritual but it is done without any sense of ownership.Â  It goes without meaning for most.Â  It is the thing to do on Sunday since it is something that has been done before.Â  But when you enter into the Anglican Church, and other Churches,Â I know that not all are like this but the vast majority are, you do not see very many young families or children running around.Â  Opps, children are not suppose to be running around the Church.Â  I had forgotten that rule.Â  Nor is any one permitted to sit in any seat that any of the parishioners have sat in for years.Â  Visitors be aware!

I see the Church as being an out reach of God.Â  We move from the four walls to meet people where they are, in their pain, their hurts, and their confusion.Â  That the people that go to Church will be people of God, they have made a personal commitment to God and accepted Jesus Christ as their Saviour and live out their lives accordingly.Â  I would like to see the Church being a place where love is not a four letter word but is something that is lived out for real.Â  That the people will, at a moment notice, be ready to give a testimony to the saving grace of God in their life.Â  The people are filled with joy.Â  They want to share their faith and blessings with others.Â  I would like to see the Church a place of comfort for the confused, rest for the weary, acceptance of the individual, and hope for a people that have no hope any longer.Â  We do not drive people away but invite them into our fellowship where they will get to know God in a very personal way.Â  And not only know him, but experience him.Â  I pray that the people will have a vision that is equal to the vision of Jesus.Â  I pray that the mission of the Church will be that of Jesus.Â  A place that is loving, caring, showing compassion and reaching out to others in need.
