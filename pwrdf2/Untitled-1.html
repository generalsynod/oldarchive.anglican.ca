Anti-Racism Working Group reviews church's commitments
The Anti-Racism Working Group of General Synod met in early December to review the commitments of the Anglican Church of Canada as set out in A Charter for Racial Justice, which was adopted by the Council of General Synod in 2007. The working group also considered the future of its work in light of reduced staff and a reduced budget of only $3,000 in 2011. Read the full highlights online.

Seeking nominations for Vision 2019 Implementation Team and Anglican Consultative Council

• Vision 2019 Implementation Team

The Council of General Synod is seeking nominations to the Vision 2019 Implementation Team. Here is the team’s draft terms of reference:

Membership:
-3 members from the Council of General Synod (COGS)
-3 others from across the church having some connection with the previous task force or members of General Synod who experienced the presentations and discussion
-The Primate and General Secretary ex officio

Meetings:
-Meet the day before or the day after COGS.

Responsibilities:
-Review the comments from table groups at General Synod on Vision 2019.
-Revise the preliminary timeline proposed as an appendix to the Vision 2019 Report into an action plan for the triennium based on consultation with officers, committees, councils and boards.
-Bring the revised action plan to the Council of General Synod for assessment and approval.
-Oversee and monitor implementation of the action plan once approved.
-In 2012, propose an action plan for the next triennium.

Accountability:
-Report through the council’s Planning and Agenda Team to the Council of General Synod, engaging members in discussions about the implementation of Vision 2019.

Liaison required with:
-the Governance Working Group as it develops its recommendations for this triennium.

• Clergy rep on Anglican Consultative Council

Stephen Andrews was elected by the Council of General Synod during the last triennium to be the clergy member on the Anglican Consultative Council (ACC). However, he has since been elected bishop of the Diocese of Algoma. Peter Elliott, who had been elected the alternate, then became the “regular” clergy member, thereby leaving his former position (as alternate member) vacant. Any priest in good standing in the Anglican Church of Canada can be nominated for this position

For a full description of the ACC, please see Appendix N in the Handbook.  

Nominations for the Vision 2019 Implementation Team, as well as for the clergy alternate to the Anglican Consultative Council will include the person’s consent to being nominated, and a brief biography (no more than half a page).Nominations may be emailed to Archdeacon Michael Pollesel, general secretary.

Continuing Education:

Churches' Council on Theological Education in Canada, Toronto, Ont. and the Canadian Theological Students’ Conference will hold their 2011 conferences at the University of Winnipeg. The two conferences, hosted by the Faculty of Theology, will run concurrently from May 14-20.

Mount Carmel Spiritual Centre, Niagara Falls, Ont., an ecumenical retreat and conference centre, has published its 2011 program of retreats and workshops.

Luther Seminary, St. Paul, Minn., offers an extensive program of courses and webcasts for 2011 through The Center for Lifelong Learning for Church Professionals and its Kairos program.

The Society For Biblical Studies, Arlington, Mass., offers pilgrimages and study tours to the Holy Land, as well as Greece and Turkey.

CEP Online is the Continuing Education Plan website to assist clergy and laity of the Anglican Church of Canada with continuing education for effective ministry. The website includes information on over 500 websites in Canada, the USA and overseas, and a variety of courses and other educational opportunities.
