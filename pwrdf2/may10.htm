With the support of Anglicans from across Canada, PWRDF makes financial and human resources available to support partners’ initiatives to improve the quality of life and address the root causes of poverty and injustice. 1. Le Tour de PWRDF

18 days, 1,300 kilometres, a 50th anniversary, a bike named Olive and a dedicated PWRDF staff member.  These are the ingredients for “Le Tour de PWRDF”, an awareness and fundraising bike ride being planned by Suzanne Rumsey, PWRDF’s Public Engagement Coordinator.  Her ride, beginning June 9 in Halifax and ending June 27 in Ste. Anne de Bellevue near Montreal, will mark the official closing of PWRDF’s 50th anniversary celebration at General Synod in Halifax.  PWRDF is encouraging Anglicans across Canada to “tour with Le Tour”. 

For information on how individuals and parishes can participate in and contribute to the ride go here. For those who are able to organize events and/or collect sponsors, please contact your diocesan representatives for donation forms. 

2. World Religious Leaders’ Summit

As we reported in the March 2010 Update, faith leaders from the Christian, Muslim, Jewish, Hindu, Sikh, Bahá’i, Aboriginal and other religious traditions will meet in Winnipeg under the leadership of Archbishop Fred Hiltz, in the week leading up to the G8/G20 summits in June.  A motion was passed at the May 5-7 meeting of the PWRDF Board of Directors endorsing the three asks of the interfaith statement: Address poverty, Care for our environment and Invest in peace.

For more information about how you can support our faith leaders go to www.faithchallenge8.com. And let us know about your plans! You may send word to Meagan Blais at mblais@national.anglican.ca.

3. PWRDF Partner in Mexico receives award

On April 24, Fr. Oscar Enriquez Perez, founder and director of PWRDF partner organization, the Paso del Norte Human Rights Centre in Ciudad Juárez, México, received the sixth annual Voice of the Voiceless Award.  The award was presented to Fr. Enriquez by Annunciation House, a migrant and refugee serving agency across the border from Ciudad Juárez,  in El Paso, Texas.  The award recognized the work of Fr. Enriquez and the Centre “on behalf of the voiceless victims of violence in Juárez” where a bloody drug war has claimed the lives of over 5,000 individuals since it began in January 2008. 

PWRDF sent a message to Fr. Enriquez, congratulating him on receiving the award.   In his response, Fr. Enriquez noted that the recognition, together with the award ceremony “was a stimulus for me to intensify my option for the victims of violence and to strengthen my commitment to the poor.  The recognition was for the whole Centre.”  Please pray for Fr. Enriquez and the staff of the Centre who risk their own lives to defend those whom they serve in Ciudad Juárez.

4. PWRDF and General Synod

Feeling the Winds of God: Charting a New Course.

Under this theme, the General Synod of the Anglican Church of Canada is meeting at St. Mary’s University in Halifax this June. PWRDF staff and volunteers will be present and joining them is

Sooriyakumary Sinnathanby, PWRDF partner from OfFER (Organization for Eelam Refugees Rehabilitation) Ceylon. Along with observing the sessions and discussions in plenary, visiting the displays area and meeting old friends and making new ones, we will be wrapping up our 18 month celebration of PWRDF’s 50th anniversary program. And what a joyful 18 months it has been. Celebration, awareness, fundraising and education were the goals and we have reached them in a variety of ways. To commemorate PWRDF’s ‘birth’ day, September 7, 1959, Anglicans participated in a hymn writing contest, produced a double CD, One Voice, enrolled in a 50 Leaders for the Anglican Church of Canada program, held local and regional fundraising events and enlivened the work of PWRDF in ways that will carry us through the next 50 years. The 50 Refugees Sponsorship initiative is more than half way there and may even surpass the goal. We give thanks for the energy and enthusiasm of Canadian Anglicans that has come to us through the 50th anniversary programs. To read more please go towww.pwrdf50.org.

5. PWRDF Seeks to Access Matching Funds from CIDA for Relief Work in Haiti

PWRDF is grateful to the tens of thousands of Anglicans across Canada who have responded through prayer, action and giving funds to allow us to respond to the devastating earthquake that struck Haiti on January 12 of this year.  To date, PWRDF has received $2,144,332 of which $1,990,141 is eligible for matching by the government of Canada through the Canadian International Development Agency (CIDA).  PWRDF is working to access the CIDA matching funds through a coalition called Canadian Churches in Action (CCA).  CCA is a coalition of 9 denominational relief and development agencies that work together to respond to humanitarian disasters.

6. KAIROS News

World Week for Peace in Israel-Palestine, May 29 to June 4 2010

For more than 40 years, Palestinians in the West Bank, Gaza Strip and East Jerusalem have been suffering under the Israeli occupation. Day-to-day life is interrupted by the separation wall, checkpoints, house demolitions, ever-expanding settlements, land seizures and inequitable water use policies. Human rights are denied through arbitrary arrests and an intricate web of bureaucratic rules and regulations limiting the most basic of freedoms. Israeli military actions and Palestinian attacks have caused far too many deaths and both sides live in uncertainty and fear.

In a time when hope seems all but gone, KAIROS joins the World Council of Churches in its worldwide call and invites congregations to take part in the World Week for Peace in Palestine Israel between May 29 and June 4, 2010. Join churches around the world in prayer and action for peace for Palestinians and Israelis. Use the KAIROS bulletin insert (PDF), which includes the Jerusalem Prayer written by the heads of the Churches in Jerusalem, in your services on Sunday, 30 May 2010.

See the World Council of Churches’ resource page for more information on how to get involved. As of May 11 KAIROS is also running an urgent action on recent military orders that would allow the Israeli Defence Forces to deport thousands of Palestinians.

We also invite you to have a look at KAIROS’ Countries of Concern: Palestine and Israel page for more information about our work in the region and our position regarding a just peace in Israel/Palestine.

7. CoCoSI wins prestigious United Nations award

PWRDF partner, the Committee Against AIDS ( CoCoSI), a youth-led initiative in rural El Salvador has been recognized by the United Nations Program on HIV and AIDS with the prestigious Red Ribbon Award.  The award is given to 25 community-based organizations from throughout the world carrying out AIDS awareness, prevention and advocacy work.  CoCoSI has been specifically recognized for its role in empowering women and girls, its struggle against poverty and stigma and its promotion of community-based health initiatives. 

Two representatives of CoCoSI have been invited to travel to Vienna, Austria in July to receive the award at the XVIII International AIDS Conference.  This international recognition comes at a time when community-based organizations in El Salvador have come under increasing threat of violence, particularly in Cabañas province where CoCoSI is based.

PWRDF joins the many partners in El Salvador and internationally that CoCoSI works with, in congratulating them on this wonderful achievement!
