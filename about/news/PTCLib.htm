Librarian Needed - Pacific Theological College

18th June 2008

Dear Partners,

Greetings from the Pacific Theological College in Suva. May God bless your work and the partnership which we enjoy.

I am writing to you today because I would like to seek your


The quad at PTC

support in our search for a librarian. The George Knight Library, named after the first principal of PTC, is the most important Theological Library in the South Pacific with approximately 31,000 volumes and more than 50 journals subscribed. It has an online public access catalogue and does not only serve the students and lecturers of PTC but also students and the general public of different theological schools in Fiji and the region. The library offers specialized resources of Pacific based theology for member churches in the South Pacific and researchers from all over the world.


The library building

The last professional librarian we had returned to Europe at the end of 2005. Since then we have worked with two library assistants. There are no local librarians available and there is no university degree in professional librarianship offered at the regional university in Fiji. All professional librarians therefore come from overseas or are trained overseas.

The quality of PTC relies on its academic lecturers and on the quality of its library services which support the academic program. After the introduction of the PhD program and the emphasis on the Master of Theology program since last year there has been a particular need for a librarian with the following professional qualifications:

(1) library management in an academic or theological library

(2) application and maintenance of an integrated library system

(3) staff supervision, training and management;

(4) development of library policies

(5) technical services and the application of international standards

(6) familiarity with online databases, the Internet and Microsoft applications

(7) guiding students in information literacy and advanced bibliographical research.
