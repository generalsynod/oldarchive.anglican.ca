PMEJ Committee Highlights

Brandon, Manitoba, April 3-6, 2008

The newly established Partners In Mission and Ecojustice Committee follows the tradition of the former, separate committees, and meets in different parts of our country – not just in Toronto. This allows committee members to see and learn about different parts of our church, to visit diocesan and parish ministries and to worship in local parishes and speak about their national committee and its work. This meeting was held in Brandon Manitoba at the invitation of the diocesan bishop, James Njegovan, and committee member Nigel Packwood.

The “PMEJ” Committee continues its work of amalgamating the Partners in Mission Committee and EcoJustice Committee.

Highlights:

  • Welcome to our new director, Henriette Thompson.
  • Invitations have been extended to two dioceses for the 2009 and 2010 Justice Camps.
  • Staff will continue to produce Praying with our partners -- next issue out soon for Pentecost – May 11-July 27, 2008.
  • Began and continued work on resources in response to requests from General Synod. These include an article on travel preparation and tourism for Anglican visitors to places such as Israel or Myanmar (Burma); an updated version of the resource Just War? Just Peace!; and one-page information sheets about different parts of the Anglican Communion with prayers for parish use.
  • Discussed the idea of (1) Justice Days, one day events on a variety of local issues along the principles of Justice Camps; and of (2) Come and See, exchange opportunities between urban centres and Indigenous communities of the North
  • Identified emerging ecojustice priorities and related task groups:
    • Climate Change and reducing CO2 emissions;
    • Homeless and Housing Advocacy Initiative;
    • mining and resource extraction by Canadian companies in Canada and internationally.
  • Continued policy and program review in the areas of Volunteers in Mission, Theological Students International Internship Program, Global Relations, Indigenous Justice.
  • Heard a presentation by Florence Ayban referring to the process of achieving financial autonomy for the Episcopal Church of the Philippines, including a voluntary clergy tithe of 10% toward the larger church. [links for more information below]
  • Heard a presentation from the Rev. Paul Gehrs, Assistant to the National Bishop for Synodical Relations, ELCIC highlighting their Stewardship of Creation initiative, and plans for a youth gathering in August 2008 in Whitehorse, Yukon on the theme of Travel Light.
  • Recommended an educational component for each committee meeting focused on a global issue with local Canadian connections, i.e., Israel/Palestine Conflict – that would involve, for example, sharing partners concerns, water issues, ethical travel practices, with KAIROS and other materials available.

Report by Cynthia Patterson on her membership on the Anglican Communion Peace and Justice Network, with a more detailed report on the most recent meeting in the Great Lakes region of Africa. PMEJ warmly thanked Cynthia for being our able representative on the network.

Farewell dinner with our former director, Ellie Johnson
