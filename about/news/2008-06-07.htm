Update from Belize on Tropical Storm Arthur

Water damage in one of the classrooms of St Paul’s

As we are well aware, our country and, in particular, those in the south, were adversely affected by the recent Tropical Storm Arthur. The heavy rains of the storm have already caused tremendous flooding in several communities and the persistent rising waters continue to pose a serious threat to these and other communities.

The national effort of the government and other agencies is fast moving toward assisting families with rebuilding their homes and repairing damages to property, in addition to meeting their immediate needs. This naturally necessitates the acquisition of building materials along with the provision of food, drinking water, clothing and other items for daily survival.

The Anglican Diocese of Belize welcomes the opportunity to play its part in assisting the people of Belize and helping our sisters and brothers to get their lives back to normal.

Damaged home in Corozal

We express our profound gratitude to all those who continue to support us in our efforts, and we thank God for his wonderful goodness and love, especially as shown in the generous response of his faithful children at this time.

Of course, monetary donations will be best as this will enable us to be more targeted in our outreach and assistance. We have already secured some funding and we are hoping to make a trip to some of the affected areas by week’s end for a preliminary assessment of how we can best assist further in the recovery efforts.

For this first visit we will be taking mainly contributions of food stuff (chiefly canned goods) and drinking water. If you wish to make contributions of canned goods and water please have them delivered to the diocesan office at the corner of Regent Street and Rectory Lane by Thursday or Friday of this week or call 227-3029 and 227-8055.

Again, thanks and God’s richest blessings.
+Philip Belize
