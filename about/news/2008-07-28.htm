Global Faith and Justice Student Conference in Montreal

August 1-9, 2008, Collège Jean-de-Brébeuf, Montreal
 
The World Student Christian Federation is delighted to announce that the official website for our upcoming General Assembly is now online.

More than 200 students from every region of the world - representing almost every denomination of the Christian church - are gathering in Montreal this August for a meeting focused on social justice, solidarity and Christian activism.
 
The 34th General Assembly of the World Student Christian Federation (WSCF) will focus its work in progressive Christian action through the theme "Your sons and daughters shall prophesy"(Joel 2:28). "In a religious landscape dominated by narrow-minded conservatism, this Assembly will be a breath of fresh air, proclaiming Christian faith as the driving force behind progressive action for justice and peace from a truly global perspective" said David Ball, Co-ordinator of the Student Christian Movement of Canada, a WSCF member movement. "A meeting of this significance will add depth and weight to the social Gospel message breaking out in North America," he said.
 
Founded in 1895, WSCF was a catalyst for the forming of the World Council of Churches.
 
The Federation of 105 national Student Christian Movements (SCMs) currently has a membership of over two million university students and Christian youth.
 
The Assembly will involve young Christians from diverse theological and cultural backgrounds. Delegates come from Africa, Asia, the Pacific, Europe, the Middle East, Latin America, the Caribbean and North America and from Anglican, Roman Catholic, Protestant, Pentecostal, Oriental and Eastern Orthodox churches.
 
Issues likely to top the Assembly's agenda are advocacy and support for people's movements in Burma/Myanmar, Zimbabwe and the Philippines and long-term peace-building through Arab Christian students in the Middle East. New movements applying for affiliation at this Assembly include Iraq and Timor Leste.
 
Keynote speeches will be presented at the conference as follows:
 
Dr Rubem Alves Brazilian Theo-poet -Forty years on - 1968's student generation
 
Rev'd Dr James A. Forbes African American preacher and rights activist of Riverside Church New York. Head of the US living wage campaign "Let Justice Roll"
 
Senator Nancy Ruth senior politician, women's and human rights campaigner, former member of SCM Canada will speak on "Empowering Visions"
 
Dr Gregory Baum celebrated Roman Catholic theologian, ecumenist and justice activist of the Montreal Jesuit Centre "Justice et foi" will speak on The meaning of prophetic religion today.
 
Rev'd Dr Patricia Kirkpatrick Associate Professor of Hebrew Bible/Old Testament studies, McGill University will speak on Biblical Prophets and Prophecies.
 
Delegates will engage in a programme of dialogue and learning with Montreal organisations. Visits will include First Nations communities, Queer - GLBT Montreal, homeless and refugee communities, the Montreal City Mission, Cadet-Roussel Organic Farm Ecology and Urban Food Program and l'Autre Montréal tours on racism and women's rights.
