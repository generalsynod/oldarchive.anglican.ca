SET & Hurricane IKE

The historic Seminario Evangélico de Teología (SET), situated in Matanzas, Cuba, trains clergy and lay leaders for ministry in Cuba’s Protestant and Episcopal churches. SET and the Anglican Church of Canada have worked in partnership for many years on issues of importance in theological education in Cuba and Canada.  An annual Partnerships grant supports Episcopal students at the seminary, as well as the Mauricio Lopez Chair in Social Analysis and Social Justice, and SET’s general operating costs. 

This letter comes to us from Dr. Reinerio Arce-Valentín , Rector of the seminary:

17 September 2008

SET chapel

Dear brothers, dear sisters,

 On behalf of our Seminary, we would like to extend to you our most heartfelt thanks for your solidarity with our people and our Church. We Cuban men and women are experiencing, at this moment, the pain for the damage, not only to thousand of homes, many of them completely destroyed, but also to schools, hospitals, theatres, and cultural heritage of the nation; as well as to crops, and production and service facilities.  It is a very painful picture. Many people have to face the loss of all their goods.

Thanks to God, human lives have been safeguarded and loss of life has been at a minimum (7 persons), taking into consideration the magnitude of this natural disaster.  We ask you to pray with us for the families of those that have lost their lives.  Likewise, we ask you to continue praying for our people and the peoples of the Caribbean that have also been affected by the battering of these two hurricanes.

In our Seminary, losses have been minimal, compared to the reality faced in many places in the rest of the country, including our own Matanzas province. Despite that all necessary measures were taken in order to protect our properties and goods, four computers were ruined, because water entered the computer classroom.  Four windows at the section of the library where books are stored came off.  Fortunately, the windows did not fall and the books were not damaged.  Nevertheless, the windows need repair.  Besides, a wall fell and the cabin of the vegetable garden where seedlings are grown was lost.  Some plantings were also lost.


Students at SET

However, we can share with you a beautiful experience in the middle of so much anguish. The Seminary housed over 50 persons, as requested by the Civil Defense, because their homes were in danger of floods or collapse. The group of persons evacuated was, mainly, sick people and pregnant women. The students that remained in our facilities and our workers took care of them all the time they sheltered in our institution.  It is the kindness of the Lord that gave us the possibility to show, with concrete deeds, the love we proclaim.

We thank our good God, once again, for your solidarity, friendship, and brotherhood.  And we pray so that all our people can feel, in such difficult times, the presence of His Holy Spirit, comforting and sanctifying, especially through our many concrete deeds of love we will try to multiply, to feed the hope that we will succeed one more time.

Fraternally in Christ our hope,

Reinerio.
