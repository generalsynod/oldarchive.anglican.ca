International Peace Conference

The Anglican Church of Canada, through its national Partners in Mission, EcoJustice and PWRDF programs, has a long history of commitment to partnership with churches and church councils in the Middle East towards peace with justice in the region. The Palestine Israel Ecumenical Forum (PIEF), to be launched in Amman, Jordan, June 17-21, 2007, initiates a process of renewed and broader ecumenical collaboration, commitment and action for peace with justice in Palestine and Israel.

The Rev. Robert Assaly, Diocese of Ottawa, is attending on behalf of the Anglican Church of Canada.Omega Bula, Executive Minister, Justice, Global & Ecumenical Relations Unit, United Church of Canada, and Mary Corkery, Executive Director, KAIROS: Canadian Ecumenical Justice Initiatives are also attending.

World Council of Churches

INTERNATIONAL PEACE CONFERENCE
"Churches together for Peace and Justice in the Middle East”

Amman, Jordan
17-21 June 2007

Aim: The conference is an event of the wider WCC constituency that initiates a process of new and broader ecumenical collaboration, commitment and action in advocacy for peace with justice in Palestine and Israel.

Objectives: The immediate goal of the conference is to launch the Palestine Israel Ecumenical Forum (PIEF). Steps needed:

a) To announce this international ecumenical advocacy initiative, invite existing and new partners to collaborate beginning with the launch meeting itself, encourage wider commitment from WCC member churches and partners to work in a coordinated way for a just peace in the region and declare intentions for joint action.

b) To listen to messages of the churches in the region, hearing their vision, experiences and challenges concerning a just peace.

c) To reaffirm, widen and strengthen ecumenical cooperation for a just peace in the region.

d) To learn from experiences in dealing with related issues in other regions.

e) To provide a synthesis of the workings of PIEF, its vision, structure and strategic framework.

Participants:

  • All 13 churches in Jerusalem (including Jordan and Israel)
  • All 10 WCC member churches in the Middle East
  • WCC member churches involved in peace and justice issues for whom the Middle East has a special interest, and churches and ecumenical organizations that are potential new partners
  • Middle East Council of Churches and all other Regional Ecumenical Organizations
  • WCC ecumenical partners
  • Specialized Ministries and agencies with a special interest in the region
  • Resource persons
  • Key NGOs working in the region
  • Representatives from international organizations (UNRWA, Council on Human Rights, etc.)
  • Church-related organizations in Palestine and Israel
