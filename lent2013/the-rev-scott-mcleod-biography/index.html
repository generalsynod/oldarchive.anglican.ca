The Rev. Scott McLeod: Biography

The Rev. Scott McLeod serves as coordinator of online ministries for the Diocese of British Columbia.

Born and raised in Victoria, B.C., Scott attended Christ Church Cathedral with his family growing up. He went to Sunday School, was a server at the altar, and participated in youth groups. Scott was also active in the B.C. Yukon Anglican Youth Movement.

In high school Scott discovered the wonders of such things as motorcycles and jazz music. Music has always been and continues to be a big part of his life.

After high school, Scott studied music at the University of Toronto, specializing in jazz. While in Toronto, he started to explore a potential call to ministry, and after much discernment, thought, and prayer, he enrolled at the Vancouver School of Theology.

At VST, Scott had two unique travel opportunities. First, he served a summer internship in the country of Seychelles, coordinated through the national Anglican Church. Later, Scott helped bring a group of youth and young adults to Guatemala where they experienced solidarity and development work.

In 2005, Scott returned to Victoria. He was ordained in June and started his curacy at St. Mary’s Oak Bay. In 2007 Scott became rector at All Saints, View Royal and in 2010 he joined the 3Saints Team Ministry project.

In addition to his current role as coordinator of online ministries, Scott also acts as coordinator for the diocesan refugee sponsorship program and sits as the bishop’s representative on the board of Threshold Housing Society.

Scott is married to Su, and they have their first son, Theo, and a dog, Feasgar. Scott enjoys being a father and husband and continues to ride, play music, and be involved in ministry inside and outside the church.

He has been told on occasion that he doesn’t fit the mould. Whatever that is.
